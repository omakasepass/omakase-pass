Ichi-go ichi-e · One time, one meeting

In Japan, omakase is built on a simple covenant: the guest entrusts the meal to the chef, and the chef answers with the best of that day: that fish, that season, that moment. The tradition carries a name for it: ichi-go ichi-e. One time, one meeting. A meal that will never happen the same way twice.

The Omakase Pass is your way in. We bring that moment to your table, matching one of a small, vetted collective of Nashville's most talented independent sushi chefs to your occasion. No restaurant reservation six weeks out. No traveling celebrity chef's calendar. Just a craftsman, your kitchen, and an evening made once… for you.

Questions, answered.

What's included in the price?

Everything but the table. Your chef brings the fish, the ingredients, and their own tools, and handles preparation, service, and cleanup. You provide the kitchen and the guests. Final quotes depend on your menu and party size.

How far in advance should I book?

As much notice as you can give us helps. Each event is matched to an individual chef's calendar, and weekends fill quickly, so earlier means more options. Once we have your date, we'll confirm a chef who fits.

What if I need to reschedule or cancel?

Plans change, and we'll always try to work with you. The exact deposit and cancellation terms are confirmed in writing when you book.
